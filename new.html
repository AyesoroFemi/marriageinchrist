DYING TO LIVE IN MARRIAGE

Marriage is a place where it can be easy to forget that it's not about getting only all our own desires met, but rather it is all about serving each other and following the example of Christ.
Husbands are admonished to love their wives as Christ loves the church (Ephesians 5:25) and "…young women to be sober, to love their husbands, to love their children,” (Titus 2:4). Jesus is our example of how to treat each other in our marriages.

The Easter season that was just concluded is always a big time of reflection of the selfless sacrifice up to the point of death Jesus, the groom, made for us his bride - the Church. It's very mind-blowing that Jesus went through all that to pay the price for our redemption. It is very difficult to find people who might consider dying for someone who is innocent and guiltless. No man will ever want to die for a sinful and hard to get along with person. However, that is what Jesus did for us.

How could He have done this? What was His motivation? The Bible made us understand that Jesus endured the cross for our sake, considering the JOY that was set before him. What JOY? The joy of the outcome of His death. The JOY of our redemption and bringing many sons to glory. We are therefore encouraged to look unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith.

We do see Jesus, who was made lower than the angels for a little while [by taking on the limitations of humanity], crowned with glory and honor because of His suffering of death, so that by the grace of God [extended to sinners] He might experience death for [the sins of] everyone. (Hebrew 12:2). For it was fitting for God [that is, an act worthy of His divine nature] that He, for whose sake are all things, and through whom are all things, in bringing many sons to glory, should make the author and founder of their salvation perfect through suffering [bringing to maturity the human experience necessary for Him to be perfectly equipped for His office as High Priest]."(Hebrews 2: 9-10 - Amplified Version). Jesus being our perfect example, we are commanded to follow his footsteps because he said , "We will do greater works than he did." (John 14:11)

While there's no need for us to die physically for anyone, there is one death that is needed, and we have been commanded to die. When the bible tells us to die in our flesh. Colossians 3:5-11 says to put to death, therefore, whatever belongs to your earthly nature: sexual immorality, impurity, lust, evil desires, and greed, ... Apostle Paul said, "I die daily."(1Corinthians 15:31). I believe Paul said this because he knew that he needed to be intentional by reminding himself every day to walk in the newness of life by "dying daily" to the flesh.

How do we die to ourselves in our relationship with our spouses? It is when we put the needs of our spouses before ours - we are being dead to flesh.

Let's look at some practical ways we can put the needs of our spouses over our own.

1. We need to first humble ourselves daily before both the Lord and our spouse and pray for God to help us serve each other with love daily.

2. We need to be quick to forgive and forget. We should always extend the grace that we received from God to our spouses as well. The easy way to forget is to stop thinking and talking or bringing the offense up from time to time. Just as God forgave us by wiping all our sins with the blood of Jesus and not making us accountable once he has forgiven us, that is the way he wants us to forgive our spouses and even others as well.

3. Appreciation - being generous in giving praise. The Bible says we should speak words that will edify one another. Some people might feel less of themselves when they offer praise to their partners. When we remind ourselves that it's a way of dying to self and edifying your partner, He will supply the courage to make it a habit. Some also might feel it may make their partners get puffed up by praising them. We need not worry about that. We just need to be obedient to the word of God and play our own part. We should refrain from playing the part of the Holy Spirit in their lives.

As time passes, and years add up, staying in love can get increasingly challenging, because responsibilities also grow. However, it's possible to still stay in love with being intentional in dying to self everyday, which will make our partners feel more connected and loved.
God uses our marriage to refine and grow us!
As a married couple, the opportunity to serve each other is presented to us daily when we keep our flesh constantly in check and in alignment with the word of God. We might occasionally mess up because we are still human, but the most important need is for us to have a willing heart and a mindset/consciousness of dying to the flesh daily. As we continue to embark on this journey, God starts to shine through us more and more; then, our marriages can be a witness for Christ and bring more glory to God.

Shalom!
