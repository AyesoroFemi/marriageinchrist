Monthly Words of Wisdom.

ANSWERING OUR MARITAL QUESTIONS.

When we get married we are given our marriage certificates before attempting the tests in the courses of our marriages. I see the certificates as questions/problems that need our ability to answer those questions correctly determines our marital successes.

Since there is no school for marriage, most of the time we start to answer our marital questions with our prior past experiences, possibly what we  see in other people's marriage before we got married, like our parents, family members, friends, culture, our own personal preferences/ideas etc. However, since God is the author of marriage, it is vital for us to have the knowledge of his laid down principles when he instituted marriage. It is very important to know that each marriage is unique with God's plan for individual marriage. Hence, using another marriage's manuscript for our own marriage is defeating God's plan and purpose for it. It is imperative   to know what the questions are in that marriage. Some are very obvious, some are deeper than on the surface and take time and revelational knowledge to discover; hence we need patience and God's help to unravel these questions in our marriages.

The next step is to ask and receive directions on how to solve these questions. Getting specific directions on how to solve these questions are very vital,  the same question in another relationship may not respond to the answer that will solve the question in your own marital relationship. That is why it's not good to compare your marriage with another marriage. This leads to frustration, because comparison is a robber of Joy. It takes intentionality to understand our questions in our marriage, we actually need to understand ourselves as we strive to understand our partners as well. 
We need to pray for the Holy Spirit to reveal to us who we are, our weaknesses and that of our partners.  Then we can now go to God to give us the strategies to apply in our relationships so our differences can complement each other, rather than be sources of conflicts. As the Bible says," And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose"- Rom. 8:28.

God wants us to grow up spiritually, but on our way to growth, he will even make our weaknesses work together for our good and his divine purposes in our marriages.

Our aim as we move forward in our marital relationship is to focus on each other's strength, and demonstrate unconditional love to our partners in the areas of their weaknesses, look for ways to help them in theses areas of weaknesses rather than being resentful and complaining about them We are not called to force growth on our partners or anyone. The bible says "owe no man anything, but to LOVE one another"- Prov. 13:18.

As we journey in our marriage, let us make LOVE our greatest quest as we unravel our questions, and address them with God's strategies and power.

Shalom!
MICM .
