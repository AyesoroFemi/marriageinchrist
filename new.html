COMMUNICATION IN MARRIAGE.

Effective communication is a great asset for every relationship. For any relationship to be successful, there is a main component that needs constant attention, and that is communication. Without effective communication, the relationship can break down and eventually fall apart. When an issue arises in a relationship, it is important to address it with the other person instead of keeping it bottled up. If you do not share your thoughts, it is likely that frustration and resentment may build towards the other person, and at any given time you may reach a breaking point which culminates into yelling, fighting, the silent treatment, or potentially parting of ways. If you nip the problem in the bud, the chances of having a happy, healthy, and successful relationship increase.
We have to address criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stone walling. These are barriers to communication.

Effective communication promotion requires some of these:

*Respectful listening and genuine interest: Try and be attentive when your spouse is speaking to you; looking straight into the eyes, paraphrasing what was said will promote effective communication.

* Send a note in a convenient format and easy to access to your spouse to reinforce your message.

* Schedule regular media free date nights; a good time to discuss issues that need to be attended to. There should be no such thing as small talk, women need to talk to realize their emotions at times, men need to be patient to allow their wives to release their emotions by expressing themselves in a loving way though; there should be no yelling, pointing of finger, and use of "you" statement should be minimized.

* Do not look at your phone while interacting with your spouse: It sends a clear message of misplaced priorities, and it is a barrier to effective listening and communication.

*Include your spouse as a Friend in all your social media lists: It keeps them in the abreast of what you share with others as well.

* Decide on a central location for important message/reminders, and dates. It may be a large calendar on the refrigerator – or a bulletin board in the kitchen – or a white-board by the front door or frequently access room. We need to put intentionality in promoting effective communication in our relationships for our marriages to thrive.
