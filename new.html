Monthly Words of Wisdom.

Rebuilding broken walls

There was a time in history, as recorded in the book of Nehemiah Ch 1, when the news came to Nehemiah that the walls of Jerusalem were broken, the gates burned down, and the people were depressed. It was so bad that it seemed there was no hope.  Both the adults and the children, even the animals were running around confused with no where to go.

When the gate of a city is burnt and the walls are broken down, that city’s defense system is down, and it will be opened to invasion from the enemy. When a city has no barriers, enemies can come in, attack, invade and hold the the people hostage, so it was at that time in Jerusalem that the people were devastated and began to cry out for help. “Wow our walls are broken, our gates are burnt down, there seemed to be no way out.” Suddenly, God made a way. Help came from unexpected sources. When God makes a way, no man can shut it. When He say yes, no man can say no.   

Similarly, your marriage relationship is like a wall around your home. Your display of love, affection, and unity are like the building blocks around your home. The more you and your spouse are in agreement in your prayers, confessions and actions, the more reinforcement you are adding to strengthen the marital walls of protection around your home. Though arguments are inevitable in marriages, they are acceptable as long as they do not lead to an unresolved or unmanageable conflict. Every fight is a crack in the walls of your marriage and home. The more fights, the more cracks. The more cracks, the weaker your marital walls around your home and children. Just like cracks lead to holes and holes leads to broken walls, broken walls leads to collapsed walls. Thus, it is with some marriages when care is not taken to arrest arguments that leads to conflicts/fights and eventual broken walls.

There are always opportunities for broken walls but once the wife and the husband can unite together to build their home to avoid cracks and avoid broken walls, then there is no limit to the extent of strength and height your marital walls can become. A nation is not built in a day, work needs to be done in trusting one another,  in building together and in making that home an habitation of love where Godly seeds can be raised in a Godly way.

Would you build like Nehemiah? Will you mend the broken walls of your marriage ? Will you pray? Will you fast? Will you seek help? Will you take time out? Because Nehemiah took time out from the king and said, “Give me some days off, I need to go build the walls of Jerusalem.” Will you take responsibility or will you point fingers? Will you take it upon yourself to build and be the voice of your home so that your gate can stand firm so that your family is protected?

PRAYER God, make me a Nehemiah of my home, I can no longer allow crises. I can’t afford broken walls and I can’t afford a separation. Lord you’ve called me as a wife to be a helper, you’ve called me as a head, and you’ve called me as a husband to love my wife as Christ loves the church.

Talk to God and tell Him your position, ask Him for the grace and the anointing to fulfill your mandate and your calling in this ministry called marriage and home. The home is a ministry by itself and you have to succeed at home to succeed outside the home. Shalom!
MICM
