Monthly Words of Wisdom.

Uniqueness of the unity in the Trinity and lessons for Christian marriages.

As we enter the last month of the year, it's a good time to reflect on the season and in relation to our lives and marriages.
This is the month our savior that was sent to redeem us was born.
Our marriages are to mirror Christ and the church relationship. However, recently I was pondering on the relationship that occurs between the Trinity, God the Father, God the Son and God the Holy Spirit.
There are lessons to learn from the Trinity relationship in our marriage that makes them work together seamlessly.

- The Trinity work together in unity; Genesis 1:26 "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth".
The Trinity work together in Collaboration and unity when God was going to create man, he involved the other 2 parties in the Trinity, when he said "let us make" man in our own image, after our likeness, which is the trio: Spirit, Soul and Body.
Marriage is thus designed to be a unity driven relationship, and in equality. 1Peter 3:7 "Ye husbands, in like manner, dwell with your wives according to knowledge, giving honor unto the woman, as unto the weaker vessel, as being also joint-heirs of the grace of life; to the end that your prayers be not hindered". Perceiving each other as joint heirs in a marital relationship will demand for treating each other with respect, valuing each other’s opinion, not making a crucial decision without consulting with the other party.

- The Trinity yield to each other’s will, Luke 22:42 "Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless, not my will, but thine, be done". As married couples we should be willing to yield our will to the other partner sacrificially. The bible encourages us that we should always watch out for the interest of the other. James 4:1 makes us understand that it's our selfish nature that causes us to fight and wants to have it our own way at all times.

-The Trinity listens to each other, “And I knew that thou hearest me always: but because of the people which stand by I said it, that they may believe that thou hast sent me.”- John 11:42. Married couples need to cultivate the habit of listening to each other, not only listening but hearing what is being said and responding accordingly.
We should always be ready to be sensitive to our partner's need.
The Trinity work together in humility; Phil 2:5-7" Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus: Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men".
Relating with each other in humility in a marital relationship is crucial to the success and bliss of the marriage. Just like Christ did not regard his equality with God as something to be arrogant or taken advantage of, in a marital relationship the same should apply. The hearts should be in a state of lowliness and tenderness.

-The unity works together to affirm and glorify themselves: John 17:5
"And now, O Father, glorify thou me with thine own self with the glory which I had with thee before the world was".
Couples need to cultivate the habit of encouraging each other by words of affirmation and amplifying the graces of God in their partners. This act gives encouragement in the marital journey and empowers us to do better in the relationship and in general.
In addition, our marriages are supposed to mirror the relationship of Jesus Christ and the church, this season as we celebrate Jesus's birth, we should reflect on his sacrificial love for the church (his bride) to redeem us from our sins, and present blameless before the father. This should be the attitude of husbands to their wives, to die in the flesh by overlooking their shortcomings and looking for ways to help them overcome their weaknesses instead of criticizing or attending to them in a condescending or contemptuous manner. The bible says a couple have become one flesh, and no one will treat their flesh in ways that are unbecoming as earlier mentioned, but instead nourish and cherish it. The expectation of Christ from the church(bride) in response to the act of his sacrificial love is that of reaction of respect as stated in Eph. 5:33 "Nevertheless let every one of you in particular so love his wife even as himself; and the wife see that she reverence her husband". Wives should find ways show respect to their husbands with the help of the Holy Spirit. The results will bring peace, harmony and bliss to the marital relationship for God's name to be glorified.
Shalom,
