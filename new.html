Monthly Words of Wisdom.

The Genesis of Marriage

The beginning of the year like this is a good time to reflect on the situation that led to the institution of marriage.
God started on the first day to create the Earth and everything in it. He made man on the 6th day, and said it was not good for man to be alone, even though everything man needed was already in place even before he was created. However, God felt that something was still missing in the life—within this he would not be complete.
That is why God said in Genesis 2:18, “It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him a help meet for him.” That was how the woman was created, and the first institution of God called marriage began.
Why marriage?
The first reason for marriage as noted by God in Gen. 2:18 is for ‘companionship’. God does not want man to be alone. He wants man to have someone he could be in a relationship with, someone he could be naked in front and not be ashamed. Meaning both parties do not have to hide their vulnerabilities from each other; instead of being ashamed, they both see each other as part of themselves as the Bible states, “They are no longer two but one.” Hence, the mindset of both parties towards each other's vulnerabilities should be to help, support, and overcome weaknesses without making the other person feel bad about the situation or exposing each other’s vulnerabilities.
Some people are lonely in their marriages because their partners do not have time for them. Keeping each other's company is part of the marriage relationship covenant. Thus, they start to drift apart, and loneliness starts to set in, and with loneliness comes moodiness, depression, and exposure to all kinds of vices, etc.
It is important to be intentional in creating time for each other on a daily or weekly basis depending on both parties’ schedules. Setting the time/day to be together, with each person suggesting mutually interesting activities is very important to sustain love. This will enhance the couple’s closeness and intimacy.
The second reason that God initiated marriage is in the last part of Gen 2: 18, which states, “I will provide a help meet for him." God initiated marriage to be able to help each other accomplish many needed tasks including daily chores, assignments, obligations including financial, parenting, and ministerial etc.
“Two are better than one because they have a good reward for their labour. For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth; for he hath not another to help him up. Again, if two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm alone?"- Ecc. 4:9-11.
This scripture explains the path to a fruitful, beneficial, productive, and safe marital life as it affects all partnerships. There are all kinds of problems, challenges, needs, and oppositions in our world, but a strong marriage partnership will help to confront such. Such couples will have a process of helping each other share the burdens; thus reducing the impact of such issues confronting them as they go through their difficult times. The overall result of having a help meet is a better quality of life. There is one life to live and we should aspire to live it well.
Sidelining one another in life choices or actions is often unfavorable because the possible contribution of the other party is absent. If often engenders strife. The reason could be due to upbringing, pride, ignorance etc. When we have that consciousness that we are no longer two, but one, we need to be intentional in involving our partners in what we do, maximizing the benefit of each other’s endowment. This will strengthen our marriages for a more favorable outcome to God's glory.
The third reason is to continue the work of procreation. God blessed Adam and Eve and said unto them, “Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it.” -Gen 1:28. God blessed man and woman in marriage to be fruitful and multiply to continue his plan of creation. This is one of the major reasons the devil fights marriage, so that he can sabotage the plan of God for the continuation of humanity before the return of our Lord Jesus Christ. God wants the marital relationship to bring forth Godly seeds that will bruise the head of the serpent (Satan).
“And did not he make one? Yet had he the residue of the spirit. And wherefore one? That he might seek a godly seed."- Malachi 4:16. God calls the seed from a covenant marriage “a godly seed”. The marriage covenant is a holy matrimony, and the expectation of the product should be godly.
To further strengthen and continue the godliness traits in the seed, God wants both partners to team up together with God's help to raise and bring the seed up in the way of the Lord. This is one of the fundamental task given by God to both partners of a covenant to bring the godly seed in the way of the Lord in order to be a world changer for God's glory.
As we journey through this year, let's be intentional in advancing God’s plan for marriage and regularly ask God for grace to keep the marriage covenant. Whatever sacrifices we have to make will pay off as we are co-laborers with God in this endeavor. God will reward all our labor of love.
Happy new Year!
Shalom.
